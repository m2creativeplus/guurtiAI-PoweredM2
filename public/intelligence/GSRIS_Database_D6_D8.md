## DELIVERABLE 6: OPPOSITION INFLUENCE MAP

### Category E: Hard Opponents (State Actors & Geopolitical Rivals)

1. **Egypt**
   * **Classification:** Primary Geopolitical Adversary
   * **Strategic Motivations:** Egypt views the Horn of Africa entirely through the lens of Nile water security and the Grand Ethiopian Renaissance Dam (GERD). Preventing Ethiopia from acquiring a naval foothold on the Red Sea via Somaliland is deemed a national security imperative. Cairo is utilizing the "One Somalia" policy as a proxy weapon to encircle Addis Ababa.
   * **Vulnerabilities:** Heavy reliance on IMF and Gulf bailouts. Its military intelligence apparatus is overextended across Gaza, Sudan, and Libya. 

2. **Turkey**
   * **Classification:** Strategic Competitor / Hostile Mediator
   * **Strategic Motivations:** Turkey views the Somaliland-Ethiopia MoU as a threat to its hegemony in Somali affairs (where it manages Mogadishu's airport, seaport, and TURKSOM base). It has signed offshore energy and naval defense pacts with the FGS.
   * **Vulnerabilities:** Ankara cannot afford to alienate Ethiopia (a major buyer of Turkish defense exports and construction contracts), forcing it into a delicate balancing act.

3. **Federal Government of Somalia (FGS)**
   * **Classification:** Direct Territorial Claimant
   * **Strategic Motivations:** Existential political survival. The HSM administration relies on anti-Somaliland nationalism to maintain a fragile coalition among warring federal member states.
   * **Vulnerabilities:** Zero *de facto* control over Somaliland. Entirely dependent on foreign troops (ATMIS/AUSSOM) for survival against Al-Shabaab.

4. **Djibouti**
   * **Classification:** Economic Saboteur
   * **Strategic Motivations:** Djibouti relies almost entirely on its monopoly over Ethiopian maritime trade. The development of Somaliland's Berbera Port—and the MoU—represents an existential economic threat.
   * **Vulnerabilities:** Massive external debt to China and a looming presidential succession crisis.

### Category F: Institutional Blockers

1. **The African Union (AU) & Peace and Security Council (PSC)**
   * **Classification:** Dogmatic Blocker
   * **Strategic Motivations:** Strict adherence to the 1964 Cairo Declaration regarding colonial borders. Fears that recognizing Somaliland will trigger a balkanization cascade (Cameroon, Nigeria, DRC).
   * **Vulnerabilities:** Deeply divided internally; Ethiopia holds massive sway as the host nation.

2. **The Arab League**
   * **Classification:** Ideological Blocker
   * **Strategic Motivations:** Views the Red Sea corridor as an exclusively Arab sphere of influence.
   * **Vulnerabilities:** A paper tiger. Key members like the UAE actively break consensus to pursue pragmatic economic investments in Somaliland.
