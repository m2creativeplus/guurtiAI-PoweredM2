# GLOBAL SOMALILAND RECOGNITION INTELLIGENCE SYSTEM (GSRIS)
**MASTER RESEARCH PROMPT FOR MAPPING SUPPORTERS, OPPONENTS, INFLUENCERS, DECISION-MAKERS, AND GEOPOLITICAL INTERESTS**

**ROLE**
Act as an elite geopolitical intelligence analyst, diplomatic strategist, parliamentary affairs expert, OSINT investigator, international law researcher, influence-mapping specialist, and strategic communications advisor.

**MISSION**
Create the most comprehensive intelligence database ever assembled regarding Somaliland's international status, recognition prospects, diplomatic allies, opponents, undecided actors, influence networks, parliamentary supporters, government stakeholders, media narratives, academic positions, and geopolitical interests.

The objective is not only to identify who supports or opposes Somaliland, but to determine WHY they hold their position, WHAT interests shape that position, WHAT costs they perceive from changing it, and WHAT opportunities exist to influence future policy.

**INTELLIGENCE FRAMEWORK**
Classify every actor into one of the following categories:
*   **CATEGORY A – ACTIVE SOMALILAND SUPPORTERS:** Publicly support recognition, self-determination, and diplomatic/economic integration.
*   **CATEGORY B – QUIET SUPPORTERS:** Privately or indirectly support engagement but avoid public endorsement.
*   **CATEGORY C – POTENTIAL SUPPORTERS:** Open to dialogue, trade, and security cooperation, but have not publicly supported recognition.
*   **CATEGORY D – NEUTRAL ACTORS:** Engage both Somaliland and Somalia without taking a public position.
*   **CATEGORY E – SOMALIA UNITY SUPPORTERS:** Publicly support territorial integrity and the FGS/AU position.
*   **CATEGORY F – ACTIVE OPPONENTS OF RECOGNITION:** Actively lobby against Somaliland recognition and diplomatic participation.
*   **CATEGORY G – HIGH-INFLUENCE DECISION MAKERS:** Capable of influencing recognition decisions regardless of public position.

**GLOBAL ACTOR DATABASE**
Identify every Head of State, Minister, MP, Ambassador, Envoy, Think Tank Expert, Academic, Journalist, Investor, Diaspora Leader, and Security Official engaging on Somaliland.
*Collect:* Identity, Position, Country, Institution, Affiliation, Bio, Website, Email, Phone, Social Media, Parliamentary records, Voting records, Horn of Africa engagements.

**KEY QUESTION ANALYSIS (Per Actor)**
1. What is their position on Somaliland?
2. How strong is their position?
3. What evidence supports this? (Statements, meetings, votes, articles, interviews, social media)
4. Has their position changed over time?
5. Who influences/advises them?
6. What political risks and strategic interests shape their position?

**INTEREST ANALYSIS**
For every government/institution identify: Why they support/oppose/remain neutral; What they gain from current policy; What they risk from changing it; Economic, military, diplomatic, regional, domestic, and international law interests.

**RECOGNITION BARRIER ANALYSIS**
Identify reasons for non-recognition, engagement opportunities, secession/territorial integrity concerns, regional security, AU/UN considerations, and domestic political factors.

**OPPOSITION INTELLIGENCE**
Map every actor supporting Somalia's territorial integrity.
*Identify:* Public arguments, voting behavior, lobbying activities, strategic/political/economic incentives, and alliance obligations.
*Distinguish between:* Actors lacking knowledge vs. Actors who disagree based on legal, geopolitical, economic, security, or precedent reasons.

**INFLUENCE NETWORK MAPPING**
Map who influences whom, which governments coordinate policy, and which think tanks, media outlets, parliamentary groups, and diaspora networks shape narratives.

**STRATEGIC ENGAGEMENT ANALYSIS**
Determine likelihood of supporting engagement, observer status, or recognition. Recommend engagement strategies, messaging, and specific diplomatic, parliamentary, economic, media, or diaspora channels.

**STANDARD OF EVIDENCE**
Every conclusion must be supported by: Official government documents, Parliamentary records, Speeches, Press releases, Published articles, Verified social media, Academic publications, or Credible media reporting.
*Distinguish clearly between:* Verified facts, Analytical assessments, Reasoned inferences, Unverified claims, and Speculation.
