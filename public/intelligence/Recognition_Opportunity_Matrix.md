# RECOGNITION OPPORTUNITY MATRIX (BASELINE)
**TARGET:** Guurti, Somaliland Parliament, Ministry of Foreign Affairs
**SUBJECT:** GSRIS Deliverable 5 (Initial Categorization & Risk Analysis)
**DATE:** June 2026

---

## CATEGORY A: ACTIVE SOMALILAND SUPPORTERS
*Actors who publicly support recognition, sovereignty, and direct diplomatic engagement.*

1. **Taiwan (Republic of China)**
   * **Interest:** Needs diplomatic allies; views Somaliland as a fellow unrecognized democratic success.
   * **Evidence:** Fully operational bilateral relations, mutual representative offices.
2. **Sir Gavin Williamson (UK Parliament - Conservative)**
   * **Interest:** Historical British obligations, Red Sea security, counter-terrorism.
   * **Evidence:** Introduced the Somaliland recognition debate in the UK House of Commons.
3. **Michael Rubin (American Enterprise Institute - US)**
   * **Interest:** US national security, containing Chinese expansion in Djibouti, rewarding democratic allies.
   * **Evidence:** Prolific academic and policy papers explicitly demanding US recognition of Hargeisa.

## CATEGORY B: QUIET SUPPORTERS
*Actors who privately support or heavily invest in Somaliland but avoid the formal UN recognition fight.*

1. **United Arab Emirates (UAE)**
   * **Interest:** Economic hegemony over Red Sea ports; military logistics.
   * **Evidence:** DP World's massive investment in Berbera Port; historical military base agreements.
2. **Ethiopia (Executive Branch)**
   * **Interest:** Strategic access to the sea; breaking reliance on Djibouti.
   * **Evidence:** The explosive 2024 MoU granting sea access in exchange for potential recognition. Ethiopia acts as the most aggressive (and volatile) wedge against the AU consensus.

## CATEGORY C: POTENTIAL SUPPORTERS
*Actors open to dialogue and security cooperation but fearful of AU blowback.*

1. **Kenya (Parliament & Intelligence Apparatus)**
   * **Interest:** Somaliland is a stabilizing buffer against Al-Shabaab; potential trade partner.
   * **Evidence:** Previous diplomatic overtures by Kenyan MPs and security officials. 
   * **Barrier:** The FGS threatens diplomatic retaliation (e.g., cutting Miraa trade, maritime border disputes).
2. **United States (Department of Defense / AFRICOM)**
   * **Interest:** Berbera airfield and port are vastly superior to congested/compromised facilities in Djibouti.
   * **Evidence:** US military inspections of Berbera facilities; NDAA mentions of Somaliland partnership.
   * **Barrier:** State Department strictly enforces the "One Somalia" policy to appease the AU.

## CATEGORY E/F: SOMALIA UNITY & ACTIVE OPPONENTS
*Actors who publicly support the FGS and actively lobby against Somaliland recognition.*

1. **Egypt**
   * **Interest:** Proxy warfare against Ethiopia over the GERD (Grand Ethiopian Renaissance Dam).
   * **Evidence:** Military pacts with Mogadishu; aggressive diplomatic demarches against the Somaliland-Ethiopia MoU.
   * **Motivation:** Geopolitical. Egypt does not inherently care about Somalia's internal politics, but weaponizes the "One Somalia" policy strictly to block Ethiopian sea access.
2. **Turkey**
   * **Interest:** Neo-Ottoman economic and military expansion in the Horn of Africa.
   * **Evidence:** Controls Mogadishu port/airport; massive military training bases (TURKSOM); offshore oil exploration deals.
   * **Motivation:** Economic/Security. Recognition of Somaliland threatens the FGS's claim to offshore resources and fragments Turkey's monopoly over Somali federal institutions.
3. **African Union (AU Secretariat)**
   * **Interest:** Preventing the "Balkanization" of African states.
   * **Evidence:** Strict adherence to the 1964 Cairo Declaration on respecting colonial borders.
   * **Motivation:** Legal/Precedent. Fear that recognizing Somaliland will trigger secessionist movements in Cameroon, Nigeria, and the DRC.

## CATEGORY G: HIGH-INFLUENCE DECISION MAKERS
*Actors capable of shifting the paradigm regardless of current public positions.*

1. **UK Foreign, Commonwealth & Development Office (FCDO)**
   * **Position:** Maintains "One Somalia" but recognizes Somaliland's democratic progress.
   * **Opportunity:** If the UK moves to recognize Somaliland, the rest of the Commonwealth will follow. 
2. **US State Department (Bureau of African Affairs)**
   * **Position:** Rigid opponent of fragmentation; fully backs the FGS.
   * **Opportunity:** Vulnerable to pressure from the Pentagon and Congressional Defense Committees if Djibouti falls further under Chinese influence.

---

### STRATEGIC SUMMARY
Somaliland’s immediate objective must be converting **Category C (Potential Supporters)** into **Category B (Quiet Supporters)** by bypassing foreign ministries and directly engaging intelligence, defense, and parliamentary apparatuses using the **GSRIS framework**.
