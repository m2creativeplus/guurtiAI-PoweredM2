**Target Donors:** European Instrument for Democracy and Human Rights (EIDHR) / Swedish International Development Cooperation Agency (Sida)
**Implementing Agency:** M2 Creative & Consulting (in strategic partnership with the Guurti Secretariat)
**Duration:** 24 Months

## 1. Executive Summary

The Republic of Somaliland stands as a beacon of de facto institutional stability and democratic resilience in the volatile Horn of Africa. The bicameral parliament, particularly the House of Elders (Guurti), has been the bedrock of this stability, effectively integrating traditional conflict resolution mechanisms with modern democratic governance. However, as regional security dynamics grow more complex, the Guurti faces significant institutional bottlenecks. 

This project aims to strengthen the operational and oversight capacities of the Guurti’s critical Foreign Affairs and Security Committees. By introducing modern policy analysis frameworks, standardizing executive oversight protocols, and establishing formal citizen feedback mechanisms, the intervention will modernize legislative workflows and deepen participatory democracy. The project aligns with EU/EIDHR and Sida strategic priorities of fostering democratic governance, supporting civil society engagement, and building resilient state institutions. 

## 2. Context & Problem Statement

For over 35 years, the Republic of Somaliland has maintained peace, internal security, and a functioning democratic system characterized by multiple peaceful transfers of power. This stability is largely anchored in the hybrid governance model of the Guurti, which commands profound grassroots legitimacy. To sustain these democratic dividends, it is imperative to invest in the technical capabilities of these proven democratic institutions.

**The Core Problem:**
While the Guurti excels in domestic conflict mediation, its formal operational capacity to conduct rigorous executive oversight—particularly regarding regional security and foreign policy—remains under-resourced. The Foreign Affairs and Security Committees currently operate with:
1. **Limited Policy Infrastructure:** An absence of standardized, data-driven methodologies for reviewing executive actions, treaties, and security policies.
2. **Ad-Hoc Constituent Engagement:** A lack of formalized, secure, and inclusive feedback mechanisms to channel the voices of civil society, marginalized communities, and traditional constituents into the legislative process.

Without robust technical systems and formal civil society integration, the committees' ability to hold the executive accountable and draft responsive legislation is constrained. This intervention bypasses geopolitical ambiguities by focusing squarely on the de facto necessity of supporting functioning democratic institutions that deliver stability, governance, and human rights to millions of citizens.

## 3. Project Objectives

**Overall Objective (Impact):**
To consolidate democratic governance, institutional resilience, and participatory democracy in the Republic of Somaliland by strengthening the legislative, oversight, and representational capacities of the House of Elders (Guurti).

**Specific Objectives (Outcomes):**
- **SO1:** To systematically enhance the technical and operational capacity of the Foreign Affairs and Security Committees to conduct evidence-based policy analysis and executive oversight.
- **SO2:** To establish, digitalize, and institutionalize formal citizen feedback mechanisms that securely connect civil society organizations (CSOs), marginalized groups, and citizens directly with legislative committees.

## 4. Theory of Change

**IF** the Guurti’s Foreign Affairs and Security Committees are equipped with modern policy analysis tools, standardized oversight protocols, and continuous capacity-building from governance architects (Output 1);
**AND IF** secure, formalized, and accessible channels for citizen feedback and civil society engagement are institutionalized within the legislative process (Output 2);
**THEN** the committees will conduct rigorous, evidence-based executive oversight and draft responsive, inclusive policies (Outcomes 1 & 2);
**BECAUSE** the institution will possess both the structural capability and the formalized constituent mandate required to scrutinize state actions effectively;
**ULTIMATELY LEADING TO** enhanced democratic governance, sustained institutional stability, and deepened participatory democracy in Somaliland (Impact).

## 6. Methodology & Implementation Plan

The project utilizes a "Design is Policy" methodology, recognizing that structured, well-designed visual systems and bureaucratic workflows generate institutional trust and systemic efficiency. 

**Phase 1: Institutional Needs Assessment & Systems Design (Months 1-4)**
- Conduct deep-dive operational audits of the Foreign Affairs and Security Committees.
- Design tailored Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs) and policy analysis templates.
- Architect the infrastructure for the Citizen Feedback Mechanism (digital portal + physical registry).

**Phase 2: Capacity Building & Training Delivery (Months 5-14)**
- Deliver specialized training modules on executive oversight, international treaty analysis, and national security auditing to Committee MPs and Secretariat staff.
- Deploy continuous mentorship models rather than one-off workshops to ensure knowledge retention.

**Phase 3: Rollout of Citizen Feedback Mechanisms (Months 12-18)**
- Launch the Citizen Feedback Portal.
- Conduct nationwide outreach campaigns in partnership with local CSOs to educate the public on how to submit legislative feedback and petitions.
- Train Guurti data clerks on processing, anonymizing, and synthesizing constituent feedback into actionable policy briefs.

**Phase 4: Monitoring, Evaluation, and Institutionalization (Months 19-24)**
- Execute external evaluations and refine workflows based on empirical usage data.
- Hand over all technical systems and frameworks fully to the Guurti Secretariat for long-term sustainability.
