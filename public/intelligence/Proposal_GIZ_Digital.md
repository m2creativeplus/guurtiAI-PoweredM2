# Grant Proposal: Digital Transformation of the Somaliland Guurti: E-Parliament & Data Governance

**Target Donor:** GIZ Digital Africa Initiative / Federal Ministry for Economic Cooperation and Development (BMZ)
**Applicant/Implementing Partner:** M2 Creative & Consulting (in partnership with the House of Elders, Republic of Somaliland)
**Project Location:** Hargeisa, Republic of Somaliland
**Project Duration:** 18 Months
**Total Requested Budget:** €485,000

## 1. Executive Summary

The Republic of Somaliland has maintained a stable, self-governing democratic architecture since 1991, underpinned by its bicameral parliament. The Upper House, the *Guurti* (House of Elders), serves as the cornerstone of this stability, playing a critical role in legislative review, conflict resolution, and the maintenance of peace. However, the Guurti currently operates on predominantly analog infrastructure, which limits institutional efficiency, restricts transparent legislative tracking, and introduces vulnerabilities regarding the preservation of institutional memory.

This project, **Digital Transformation of the Somaliland Guurti: E-Parliament & Data Governance**, seeks support from the GIZ Digital Africa Initiative to modernize the Guurti’s operational capacity. By implementing secure E-Parliament infrastructure, deploying a customized digitized legislative tracking system, and delivering comprehensive capacity-building in data governance for Elders and secretariat staff, this intervention will safeguard Somaliland’s legislative heritage while accelerating its integration into the digital era. The project aligns seamlessly with BMZ’s core strategic priorities of fostering good governance, institutional resilience, and digital inclusion in Africa.

## 2. Context & Problem Statement

For over three decades, the Republic of Somaliland has demonstrated remarkable institutional resilience and democratic maturity in a volatile region, conducting successive peaceful transfers of power. The Guurti is central to this de facto stability, acting as the ultimate arbiter in traditional and national disputes while fulfilling its constitutional legislative duties.

Despite its indispensable role, the Guurti faces systemic administrative bottlenecks:
*   **Analog Vulnerability:** Legislative drafting, amendments, and committee reports are processed manually, risking data loss, degradation of physical archives, and delayed inter-chamber communication.
*   **Information Silos:** The absence of a centralized, digitized legislative tracking system hampers the ability of lawmakers, civil society, and the public to monitor the lifecycle of bills efficiently.
*   **Capacity Deficits in Digital Governance:** While there is strong political will to modernize, the Elders and the administrative secretariat lack the requisite digital literacy, data protection frameworks, and cybersecurity awareness to transition safely to an E-Parliament model.

Recognizing that institutional legitimacy relies heavily on transparency and operational efficiency, bypassing traditional analog constraints is an urgent democratic necessity. Modernizing the Guurti will not only fortify Somaliland’s internal governance but also serve as a blueprint for harmonizing traditional leadership structures with 21st-century digital governance.

## 3. Project Objectives

### Overall Objective (Impact)
To enhance the institutional capacity, transparency, and legislative efficiency of the Somaliland Guurti through comprehensive digital transformation, resilient infrastructure, and robust data governance frameworks, thereby consolidating democratic stability.

### Specific Objectives (Outcomes)
1.  **Infrastructure Modernization:** To design, procure, and implement secure, scalable E-Parliament hardware and localized network infrastructure within the Guurti compound.
2.  **Workflow Digitization:** To develop and deploy a bespoke, secure Digital Legislative Tracking System (DLTS) for the archiving, drafting, and lifecycle tracking of parliamentary bills and committee reports.
3.  **Institutional Capacity Building:** To train and empower the Guurti Elders and Secretariat staff in foundational digital literacy, data sovereignty, and robust data governance protocols.

## 4. Theory of Change

**IF** the Somaliland Guurti is equipped with secure E-Parliament hardware and localized network infrastructure, **AND** provided with a customized Digital Legislative Tracking System, **AND** its Elders and Secretariat staff receive targeted, culturally contextualized capacity-building in data governance and digital literacy; 

**THEN** the legislative, review, and conflict-resolution processes will become significantly more efficient, secure, and transparent, **BECAUSE** the institution will transcend analog bottlenecks, mitigate the risk of institutional memory loss, and operationalize data-driven decision-making. 

**ULTIMATELY**, this will contribute to the strengthening of democratic governance, institutional legitimacy, and the sustained peace architecture of the Republic of Somaliland.

## 6. Methodology & Implementation Plan

The project will employ an **Agile, Human-Centered Design methodology**, ensuring that the technology implemented is culturally appropriate and strictly tailored to the specific legislative workflows of the Guurti.

*   **Phase 1: Inception & Needs Assessment (Months 1–3)**
    *   Conduct baseline IT and infrastructure audits at the Guurti.
    *   Engage parliamentary stakeholders to map out current analog workflows.
    *   Finalize software architecture requirements and data sovereignty guidelines.
*   **Phase 2: Infrastructure Deployment & Customization (Months 4–9)**
    *   Procure and install secure servers, workstations, and localized network infrastructure.
    *   Develop the Digital Legislative Tracking System (DLTS) using secure, open-source frameworks to ensure long-term sustainability.
    *   Establish a secure internal parliamentary intranet.
*   **Phase 3: Capacity Building & Change Management (Months 10–15)**
    *   Launch contextualized digital literacy workshops for the Elders (incorporating accessible UI/UX principles for older demographics).
    *   Conduct specialized Data Governance, cybersecurity, and system administration training for the Secretariat IT staff.
    *   Initiate the digitization of the legacy physical archives.
*   **Phase 4: Monitoring, Evaluation, & Handover (Months 16–18)**
    *   Conduct rigorous security penetration testing on the DLTS.
    *   Perform a comprehensive project evaluation against LogFrame indicators.
    *   Execute formal handover of systems and operational manuals to the Guurti Administration to ensure institutional ownership.
