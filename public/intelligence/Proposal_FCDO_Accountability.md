**Submitted to:** Foreign, Commonwealth & Development Office (FCDO) / Westminster Foundation for Democracy (WFD)
**Applicant:** M2 Creative & Consulting (in partnership with the Somaliland Parliament)
**Project Location:** Hargeisa, Republic of Somaliland
**Proposed Duration:** 24 Months
**Estimated Budget:** £450,000

---

## 1. Executive Summary

The **Somaliland Parliamentary Accountability & Anticipatory Governance Programme (SPAAG)** is a strategic capacity-building initiative designed to modernize the legislative and oversight functions of the Republic of Somaliland’s bicameral parliament (the House of Representatives and the House of Elders/Guurti). As the Horn of Africa faces compounding crises—chiefly climate-induced resource conflicts and emerging macro-economic pressures—the need for proactive, evidence-based legislative oversight has never been more urgent. 

SPAAG seeks to transition the parliament from a reactive institution to a practitioner of **Anticipatory Governance**. The programme focuses on two critical, interlinked pillars: 
1. Integrating anticipatory governance frameworks to mitigate and mediate climate-induced conflicts (pastoralist displacement, water scarcity).
2. Establishing robust, transparent mechanisms for public debt oversight to ensure long-term fiscal sustainability.

By strengthening the institutional architecture of Somaliland's democratic bodies, this programme aligns directly with the FCDO and WFD’s mandate to support inclusive, accountable, and resilient democratic institutions.

## 2. Context & Problem Statement

### 2.1 The Anchor of Stability in the Horn of Africa
For over three decades, the Republic of Somaliland has maintained de facto institutional stability, holding successive democratic elections and sustaining a peaceful transfer of power. Somaliland’s unique bicameral system—blending representative democracy (House of Representatives) with traditional conflict-resolution architecture (Guurti)—has been the bedrock of this stability. However, the preservation of this democratic dividend requires continuous institutional modernization. While Somaliland's international juridical status remains unresolved, its de facto statehood necessitates robust governance mechanisms to deliver public goods and maintain regional security.

### 2.2 The Dual Threat: Climate-Security Nexus and Fiscal Vulnerability
Somaliland faces two existential governance challenges that require immediate parliamentary intervention:

1. **Climate-Induced Conflicts:** Severe, recurring droughts and desertification have decimated pastoralist livelihoods, leading to forced urbanization and resource-based conflicts over water and grazing lands. The parliament currently lacks the anticipatory governance tools required to forecast, legislate, and budget for climate-security risks before they escalate into violence.
2. **Public Debt & Fiscal Transparency:** As Somaliland engages in major infrastructure development (e.g., the Berbera Port corridor) and navigates global economic shocks, public financial management is under strain. The House of Representatives lacks a formalized, technically equipped mechanism to oversee sovereign debt, scrutinize loan agreements, and ensure transparent public borrowing.

Without targeted capacity-building, the parliament risks being sidelined in critical national policy decisions, weakening democratic accountability.

## 3. Project Objectives

### Overall Objective
To strengthen the institutional capacity of the Somaliland Parliament to exercise effective democratic oversight, promote fiscal transparency, and implement anticipatory governance models to address climate-security risks.

### Specific Objectives
1. **Anticipatory Governance:** To equip the House of Elders (Guurti) and the House of Representatives with strategic foresight tools and legislative frameworks to proactively manage and mitigate climate-induced conflicts.
2. **Public Debt Oversight:** To establish and operationalize a dedicated Public Debt Oversight Mechanism within the House of Representatives, enhancing fiscal accountability and sustainable public financial management.

---

## 4. Theory of Change

**IF** the Somaliland Parliament is equipped with anticipatory governance frameworks and robust public debt oversight tools, 
**AND** Members of Parliament (MPs) and committee secretariats are technically trained in the climate-security nexus and fiscal transparency, 
**AND** institutional linkages are fostered between the legislature, civil society, and the executive branch,
**THEN** the parliament will be able to proactively address resource-based conflicts and ensure sustainable, transparent public financial management,
**LEADING TO** enhanced democratic resilience, institutional legitimacy, economic stability, and sustained peace in the Republic of Somaliland.

## 6. Methodology & Implementation Plan

The SPAAG programme will utilize a highly targeted, peer-led methodology, leveraging WFD’s global expertise in parliamentary strengthening alongside local contextual knowledge.

### 6.1 Core Activities
1. **Technical Assistance & Drafting:** 
   - Deploying governance architects to draft the *Somaliland Parliamentary Public Debt Oversight SOP*.
   - Developing a contextualized *Anticipatory Governance & Climate Security Playbook* for the Guurti.
2. **Capacity Building Seminars:**
   - Conducting intensive workshops on macro-economic indicators, debt sustainability analysis (DSA), and climate foresight methodologies.
3. **Peer-to-Peer Exchanges:**
   - Facilitating virtual and in-person knowledge exchanges with UK Parliament Select Committees (e.g., Public Accounts Committee, Environmental Audit Committee) and regional democratic peers.
4. **Multi-Stakeholder Dialogues:**
   - Hosting quarterly roundtables bridging MPs, the Ministry of Finance, the Ministry of Environment, and civil society to build consensus on fiscal and climate policies.

### 6.2 Phased Implementation
- **Phase 1: Inception & Baseline Assessment (Months 1-3):** Needs assessment, stakeholder mapping, and finalizing training curricula.
- **Phase 2: Institutional Architecture & Drafting (Months 4-9):** Drafting SOPs, forming task forces, and establishing the Debt Oversight framework.
- **Phase 3: Capacity Building & Rollout (Months 10-18):** Intensive training for MPs and secretariat staff; peer exchanges.
- **Phase 4: Consolidation & Evaluation (Months 19-24):** Mentorship during live parliamentary sessions, final evaluation, and handover.
